Meet the DJs

Derek Tang

Los Angeles, CA

Derek is a DJ, teacher, and organizer based in Los Angeles, known for his dynamic presence both behind the booth and on the dance floor. His DJing is energetic and engaging, creating momentum that keeps dancers moving and fully immersed in the music.

With a background in teaching and competition, Derek brings a clear understanding of what dancers need—crafting sets that support expression, connection, and those moments when everything just clicks.

Georges Naasan

New York, NY

Georges has been part of tango for over two decades, with roots that trace back to building one of the very first tango communities in Beirut. Since then, his journey has taken him across the U.S. and beyond—as a dancer, DJ, and community builder.

As a DJ, Georges has a gift: he creates energy that's contagious. There's a sense of joy, momentum, and connection in his music that fills the room and keeps the floor alive.

And if you know him, you know—he's just a genuinely lovely human, which somehow always comes through in his sets.

Kim Dai

New York, NY

Kim was initially drawn to tango by the music, before falling in love with the dance itself. She is inspired by the wide range of emotional expression that tango allows for.

As a DJ, she thinks of each set as a personal journey, using music to create an arc that moves dancers from the familiar into something deeper: a more conscious connection to themselves, their partner, and the shared experience on the floor. She finds it magical when the music enters the embrace and flows through the couple.

Kim runs Gotham Tango, a project dedicated to sharing tango with more people in NYC.

Ramu Pyreddy

Washington, DC

Ramu fell in love with tango music in 1999 and started dancing in 2000. To share that love and budding passion, in 2000, he started the Michigan Argentine Tango Club at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. 26 years later, he still remains deeply in love (if anything it has only grown stronger). Along the way, he has become one of the most knowledgeable and sought after tango DJ’s in the US. He has DJd at countless events – milongas, festivals and marathons – all over the US, in Argentina, Australia, Canada, and India. For the last 15+ years, he has been the host and resident DJ at Milonga Zandunga, one of DC’s popular weekly milongas, known for it’s outstanding music. As a DJ, Ramu plays mostly music from the Golden Age and has a strong preference for the Big Four Orchestras

Sabah Chammas

San Diego, CA

Sabah got addicted to Tango in the late nineties. It started with dancing and now it includes being a DJ. “…Tango music occupies my thoughts and energizes my spirit and I am in constant search of good music. Although I try to vary the music I play, please feel free to consider me old fashioned as I love Golden age music. The emotional impact of the music is what I look for in addition to the musicality.”

Ayano Yoneda

San Francisco, CA

Ayano is deeply rooted in tango—through the music, the poetry, and the culture. DJing in the Bay Area and internationally including her yearly pilgrimages to Buenos Aires, where her connection to the music continues to deepen.

She's also the co-founder of the Tango Poetry Project, reflecting her love for the lyrics and the stories behind the music. That curiosity shows in her DJing—thoughtful, intentional, and rich with meaning.

Beyond that, Ayano is a true community builder. Whether it's gathering dancers on the floor or over late-night tacos after a milonga, she creates spaces where people connect and stay connected. A little no-nonsense, a little tango nerd (in the best way), and deeply dedicated to the heart of tango.
